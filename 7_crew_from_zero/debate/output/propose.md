Boca Juniors is undeniably more significant than River Plate for several compelling reasons that highlight its cultural, historical, and sporting impact. 

Firstly, Boca Juniors enjoys a larger fan base, with millions of supporters not only in Argentina but all over the world. The iconic La Bombonera stadium, known for its passionate atmosphere, symbolizes the club’s rich history and fervent loyalty of its fans. 

Secondly, in terms of trophies, Boca Juniors boasts an impressive collection, including numerous domestic league titles and international championships, particularly its success in the Copa Libertadores, where they lead with 6 titles compared to River Plate's 4. This consistent performance on the continental stage showcases Boca's ability to compete at the highest level.

Additionally, Boca Juniors holds a unique cultural significance, representing the working-class ethos of Argentina, particularly in the popular neighborhoods of Buenos Aires. This environment breeds intense loyalty that transcends mere sports allegiance, creating a deeply rooted identity synonymous with the Argentine spirit.

Moreover, the classic rivalry, known as the Superclásico, highlights Boca's prominence, drawing unmatchable attention locally and internationally. The matches encapsulate the deep-seated passion and pride associated with Boca Juniors, making it a cornerstone of Argentine football.

In conclusion, the combination of a larger global following, greater success in coveted tournaments, and cultural relevance firmly establishes Boca Juniors as the larger club, making it not just a football team but a symbol of Argentine identity. Boca Juniors es más grande que River Plate.
